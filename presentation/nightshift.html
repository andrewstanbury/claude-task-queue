the one idea

Delegation that escalates properly.

Think of it as a tireless junior team with one unbreakable habit: it never guesses on anything important. It works through your list on its own — and the moment a piece of work looks risky, it stops working that piece and escalates it, without stopping everything else.

01 · it runs

Around the clock

Give it a list of outcomes in plain language. It works through them one at a time, checks its own work, and doesn't wait for anyone. Eight idle hours become eight working hours.

02 · it escalates

Risk goes to a person

Work that's too big, too uncertain, or missing context it can't invent is never attempted. It's handed back to you as a short set of questions — your answers turn it into small, safe steps that go back on the list.

03 · you decide

Minutes, not meetings

You review a tray of escalated calls — each one framed, with a recommendation — and a pile of finished, checked work. Confirm, redirect, done. About twenty minutes a morning.

why it's safe to leave on

Only safe work runs unattended. By rule, not by hope.

The reason you can leave it running isn't trust in the AI's judgment — it's the fence around it. Three rules, all structural:

Small, reversible steps only

The working list only ever holds small, checkable, easy-to-undo pieces. Anything bigger gets broken down first — with your input — before it's allowed on the list.

Risky work can't be started

A piece flagged as risky sits with you, not the machine. It cannot be picked up again until a person has answered the questions and re-scoped it. No exceptions overnight.

Nothing reaches customers alone

Everything it produces lands in a review area. It cannot publish, ship, or spend on its own — a person always signs the final step. That's a hard rule, not a setting.

the return

It doesn't make people faster. It moves the hours.

The work it does no longer needs to happen during someone's workday. Your team's judgment is the scarce resource — this spends it only on the escalated calls, and lets the clock do the rest.

⚠ The honest limits

  • JUDGMENT, NOT A GUARANTEE

    It escalates the risk it recognizes. That's why the fence exists: even a missed call lands in a review area a person signs off — it can't reach customers on its own.

  • GARBAGE IN

    Output quality tracks the clarity of the ask. A vague list at midnight is wasted machine hours by morning. It rewards clear direction; it doesn't replace it.
