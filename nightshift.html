the one idea

Delegation that escalates properly.

A tireless junior team with one unbreakable habit: it never guesses on anything important. The moment a piece of work looks risky, it stops working that piece and escalates it — without stopping everything else.

01 · it runs

Around the clock

Give it a list of outcomes in plain language. It works through them one at a time, checks its own work, and doesn't wait for anyone. Eight idle hours become eight working hours.

02 · it escalates

Risk goes to a person

Work that's too big, too uncertain, or missing context is never attempted. It comes back to you as a short set of questions — your answers turn it into small, safe steps that go back on the list.

03 · you decide

Minutes, not meetings

You review a tray of escalated calls — each framed, with a recommendation — and a pile of finished, checked work. Confirm, redirect, done. About twenty minutes a morning.

why it's safe to leave on

Only safe work runs unattended.
By rule, not by hope.

Small, reversible steps only

The working list only ever holds small, checkable, easy-to-undo pieces. Anything bigger gets broken down first — with your input — before it's allowed on the list.

Risky work can't be started

A piece flagged as risky sits with you, not the machine. It cannot be picked up again until a person has answered the questions and re-scoped it. No exceptions overnight.

Nothing reaches customers alone

Everything it produces lands in a review area. It cannot publish, ship, or spend on its own — a person always signs the final step. A hard rule, not a setting.

your context, plugged in

It works from your reality,
not its guesses.

Connect the systems your org already trusts — the wiki, the tickets, the design system, the data — and it reads from them while it works.

plug in

Your systems, not ours

You choose what to connect and what stays off-limits. Nothing is built in, nothing is assumed — an org adds its own context; an individual adds theirs.

consult first

Checks before it guesses

When a connected system covers a question, it looks there before inventing an answer — and when it escalates a risk to you, the questions name where the answer likely lives.

truth stays home

Your project keeps the record

What it learns that matters gets written into the project's own living record — decisions, behaviors, must-holds — so the truth about your product lives with your product.

Connections use MCP (Model Context Protocol) — the open standard your AI tooling already speaks. No custom integration layer.

straight talk

Three things a vendor wouldn't tell you.

  • JUDGMENT, NOT A GUARANTEE

    It escalates the risk it recognizes. That's why the fence exists: even a missed call lands in a review area a person signs off — it can't reach customers on its own.

  • GARBAGE IN

    Output quality tracks the clarity of the ask. A vague list at midnight is wasted machine hours by morning. It rewards clear direction; it doesn't replace it.

  • UNPROVEN UNTIL PILOTED

    The numbers on the last slide are illustrative. They describe how the workflow behaves, not a benchmark from your team. The only figures that should convince you are your own.

the bottom line

Routine work happens while you sleep.
Risky work waits for a person. Always.
